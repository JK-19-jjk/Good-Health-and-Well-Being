Sleep Basics: Why Sleep Matters

Sleep is not just a time when your body shuts down. It's an active period when important processing, restoration, and strengthening occurs. Just like eating well and exercising, good sleep is a fundamental component of overall health.

Quality sleep is essential for both physical and mental health

Adults need 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night, while teenagers need 8-10 hours and children need even more. Despite the importance of sleep, many people struggle to get enough rest, with an estimated 50-70 million Americans suffering from some form of sleep disorder.

Benefits of Good Sleep

Proper sleep improves virtually all aspects of health:

  • Enhanced cognitive function and memory
  • Improved mood and emotional regulation
  • Stronger immune system
  • Better physical performance
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases

Many people don't realize that inadequate sleep is linked to serious health problems including heart disease, diabetes, obesity, and depression. For more details, see our section on sleep disorders.

Quick Sleep Tips

  • Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night
  • Keep a consistent sleep schedule
  • Create a relaxing bedtime routine
  • Optimize your sleep environment
  • Limit screen time before bed
